Defense

The team with possession is attacking, if they are not in control of the ball it's not an attack but still defense.

1st man:

  • Make 2nd man’s job easier
  • Make it hard on enemies on ball
  • Don’t throw yourself at ball (don’t leave your 2nd man in 1v2)

2nd man:

  • Create time for your 1st man to become 2nd man
  • Should use things that bring you the advantage: Take time, Fake challenges, good low 50s…
  • Don’t go if you don’t need to
  • Cover teammates weak outcome

1 should often be backboard (1st man or 2nd man?)

Counter Attacking

Works because people overextend on offence because they don’t want to play defense.

You want to attack as quickly as possible, push the pace of the ball and make sure the last touch is perfect (take quick 1st touch).

You can shoot if they are too close a lot.

Clear it so it goes in the air.

When countering you can demo last if it's 1v2 or pass teammate if available.

When doing it go toward the net and not walls or corners.

Attack

Don’t shoot at goalies.

If you pass don’t chase your own pass, but power shooting is important.

1st man:

  • Beat their 1st man
  • Make it awkward for their 2nd man

2nd man:

  • If 1st has 2 players to beat cover his weak (50 / outcome)
  • If 1st has 1 player to beat cover his strong
  • If your 1st is attacking you must be there with him even on low boost (collect some pads, 24 is all you need) (doesn’t mean overcommit)

Rotations and Recovery’s

Part of recovering and rotating is selecting pads:

That’s what makes you a fast player

Recovery’s:

  • Recoveries are really important to get back as fast as possible but you should use as little/0 boost recovering.
  • You should try to save as much boost recovering and defending as possible
  • If you're close to supersonic speed feel free to use it.
  • With saving the boost recovering you have boost for offense

Rotations:

1st man:

  • If opponents are dribbling /on the ground, go harass them to make it easy for the 2nd man.
  • If they are in the air, it depends:
    • If you are fast enough rotate behind your 2nd man so that he can challenge from better position, and you can cover behind him.
    • If you won’t be fast enough, challenge in the air so you make it easy for your 2nd man.

2nd man:

  • If 1st has 2 players to beat cover his weak (50 / outcome)
  • If 1st has 1 player to beat cover his strong
  • If your 1st is attacking you must be there with him even on low boost (collect some pads, 24 is all you need) (doesn’t mean overcommit)

Speed and decision making

Speed:

  • Try to always keep your momentum going / don’t break check
  • If you miss a boost, go past it most of the time (depending how much momentum you have)
  • How do you become fast: work on hitting the ball as fast as possible of the reads
  • Every time it hits a surface try to hit it somewhere with intention as fast as possible (in freeplay)
  • If you feel like you are out of control, that is good because you will learn to get that speed under control
  • A lot of players do: Try to be supersonic -> break check -> try to go supersonic again

Decision making:

  • Do not hesitate
  • If you make a decision do that decision and learn. Even if it's wrong you will learn from it
  • If you make the right decision and it’s a second late because you overthought it, it’s a wrong decision -> commit to your decision as soon as possible

Efficient challenges

If you feel like you did the right challenge and you still lost it -> Think about a different angle to approach that challenge

Dive challenge:

  • Only dive challenge if you're confident it will benefit your team
  • Don't do it too often

Drive challenge:

  • Useful because you can challenge without jumping
  • Drive challenge often happens when challenge needs to happen but you don't want to throw yourself out of the play -> intention is hit ball and head back

Fake challenge:

  • Do it when you are in position of power
  • You should cover your net when fake challenging
  • I like to do it if I’m shadowing and opponent has good control of the ball
  • If you force them to do something with fake – you are now in control
  • Always cover your net when faking
  • You can fake challenge when they are panicking it will force even more panic

Carry in solo Q

Always try to make it easy for your 2nd man.

Stick to fundamentals.

If you have an awkward challenge and your teammate has a less awkward challenge let him do it.

Make it easy for both of you as a team

Cover your teammates weaknesses and enable his strengths.

If your teammate is questioning what he should be doing that’s your fault for not making it easy enough on him.

Adapting:

Positionally there is not much to adapt just follow the fundamentals.

You should mostly only be adapting to your opponents with your mechs.

Example: they prejump you when you go for air dribble -> you go back down and not air dribble into them.

Positive mental

Mental is very important in this game.

Tilting is an emotion and it's ok to tilt, you will 100% tilt if you are a competitive person.

You should try to notice enemy’s mistakes and not your teammates.

  • If you look for enemy’s -> you are looking for how to win
  • If you look for teammates mistakes -> you will see how you are losing

If you already had a bad day don’t go and play ranked, don’t do that to yourself.

If you really want to go casual first, then decide what you want.

You can make yourself play a certain number of games a day

  • You realize you have to play let say 15 games a day so you can't tilt on game 6
  • Over time your mental will get better

If your teammate is throwing the game on purpose, throw the game 2 times as hard be petty so it helps your mental, it’s a mental refresh.

Try to have fun -> you will become more creative

More detailed tips

“Sometimes fundamentals will be broken because of the situation” – iBeachz

If you want to break them you must know them first.

If there is nothing you can do, go to where you think the next play will be or do something productive (collect boost pads, get better position…)

Have intention:

  • Say I’m gonna go wall -> ceiling get reset -> get 1 touch on ball -> shoot
  • Do/try to do that don’t switch up mid shot

Break mechs down into small steps / drills

Don’t just reset your shot if you mess up try to follow it up

  • Think why you messed up, try to correct it next attempt
  • Adapt to your mistakes in training and freeplay
