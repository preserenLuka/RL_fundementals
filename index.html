Defense

The team with possession is attacking, but if they’re not in control of the ball, it's still defense, not an attack.

1st Man:

  • Make the 2nd man’s job easier.
  • Put pressure on the enemies handling the ball.
  • Avoid throwing yourself at the ball unnecessarily (don't leave your 2nd man in a 1v2 situation).

2nd Man:

  • Create time for your 1st man to rotate and become the 2nd man.
  • Utilize strategies like taking time, faking challenges, or solid low 50s for advantage.
  • Avoid challenging unless necessary.
  • Cover your teammate’s weak outcomes.
  • Positioning on the backboard is often a good idea.

Counter Attacking

Counter-attacks work because opponents overextend on offense—they don’t want to play defense.

Attack as quickly as possible. Push the pace of the ball, and ensure the final touch is clean (take a quick 1st touch).

Frequently, if they’re too close, you can go for a shot.

Clearing the ball high in the air is a good option.

During counters, demo the last player if it’s a 1v2 situation or pass to your teammate if they’re in a good position.

When countering, aim towards the net, not the walls or corners.

Attack

Don't shoot directly at goalies.

If you pass, don't chase your own pass, but remember that power shooting is still crucial.

1st Man:

  • Beat the opponent's 1st man.
  • Create awkward situations for their 2nd man.

2nd Man:

  • If the 1st man has 2 players to beat, cover his weak side (e.g., 50/50s or outcomes).
  • If the 1st man has 1 player to beat, cover his strong side.
  • If the 1st man is attacking, you should be with him, even on low boost (collect some pads; 24 boost is enough). But don’t overcommit.

Rotations and Recoveries

Selecting Boost Pads:

Pad management is key to fast rotations and maintaining speed during recovery.

Recoveries:

  • Recover quickly, but try to use as little to no boost as possible.
  • Save as much boost during recovery and defense, so you have more for offense.
  • Feel free to use boost if you are close to supersonic speed.

Rotations:

1st Man:

  • If opponents are dribbling or playing on the ground, harass them to make it easier for the 2nd man.
  • If they’re in the air, it depends:
    • If fast enough, rotate behind the 2nd man to challenge from a better position and cover him.
    • If not fast enough, challenge in the air yourself to make it easier for the 2nd man.

2nd Man:

  • If the 1st man has 2 players to beat, cover his weak side (e.g., 50/50s or outcomes).
  • If the 1st man has 1 player to beat, cover his strong side.
  • Always be ready to support the 1st man, even on low boost. (Pick up pads; 24 boost is enough).

Speed and Decision Making

Speed

  • Always try to maintain your momentum; avoid unnecessary break checks.
  • If you miss a boost, most of the time continue moving past it (depending on your momentum).
  • To become fast, focus on hitting the ball as quickly as possible based on your reads.
  • Each time the ball hits a surface, try to hit it with intention as quickly as possible (practice in freeplay).
  • If you feel out of control while speeding, that’s good—it will help you learn to control that speed.
  • A common mistake: many players aim to reach supersonic, break check, and then try to hit supersonic again.

Decision Making

  • Do not hesitate in your decisions.
  • If you commit to a decision, follow through with it and learn from the result—even if it’s wrong, you’ll learn.
  • Making the right decision late because of overthinking is equivalent to a wrong decision—commit as quickly as possible.

Efficient Challenges

If you feel like you did the right challenge and still lost it, think about approaching that challenge from a different angle.

Dive Challenge

  • Only dive challenge if you're confident it will benefit your team.
  • Don't do it too often.

Drive Challenge

  • Useful because you can challenge without jumping.
  • Drive challenges often happen when you need to challenge but don't want to throw yourself out of the play. Your intention should be to hit the ball and then head back.

Fake Challenge

  • Do it when you are in a position of power.
  • Always cover your net when fake challenging.
  • It’s effective if you're shadowing and the opponent has good control of the ball.
  • If you force the opponent to act with a fake challenge, you take control.
  • Fake challenges can increase panic, especially when they are already under pressure.

Carry in Solo Q

Team Coordination

Always try to make it easy for your second man.

Stick to the fundamentals.

If you have an awkward challenge and your teammate has a less awkward challenge, let him do it.

Make it easy for both of you as a team.

Cover your teammate's weaknesses and enable his strengths.

If your teammate is unsure of what to do, that's your fault for not making it easy enough on him.

Adapting to Situations

Positionally, there’s not much to adapt — just follow the fundamentals.

You should mostly be adapting to your opponents' moves with your mechanics.

Example: If they prejump when you go for an air dribble, adjust by going back down instead of air dribbling into them.

Positive Mental

Mental is very important in this game.

Tilting is an emotion, and it's okay to tilt — you will 100% tilt if you are a competitive person.

Try to notice the enemy’s mistakes, not your teammates’.

  • If you focus on the enemy's mistakes, you're looking for how to win.
  • If you focus on your teammates' mistakes, you'll only see how you're losing.

If you’ve had a bad day, don’t jump into ranked games — don’t do that to yourself.

If you really want to play, go casual first, then decide what you want.

Set a number of games to play each day.

  • For example, if you plan to play 15 games a day, you can't tilt by game 6.
  • Over time, your mental game will improve.

If your teammate is intentionally throwing, you can throw the game twice as hard to reset mentally. It’s a mental refresh.

Try to have fun — this will help you become more creative.

21 Advanced Tips

Mentality Tips

  • Keep a Strong Mental: Don’t let mistakes get you down; focus on the next play.
  • Avoid Autopilot: Improvement requires active effort; don’t rely on automatic actions.
  • Embrace Ranked Play: Keep grinding ranked games even if you’re not performing well; it contributes to improvement.
  • Avoid Desperation: Desperation can lead to losing more games.

Mechanical Tips

  • Use Mechanics Wisely: Don’t force mechanics; use them when appropriate.
  • Power Shooting: Focus on improving power shooting; it’s highly effective.
  • First Touches: Work extensively on your first touches to enhance your mechanics.
  • Utilize Environment: Get comfortable using walls and ceilings to your advantage.
  • Boost Through Aerial Plays: Use boost effectively in the air to improve aerial plays.
  • Fast Dribbling: Push the ball ahead of you in open space to maintain pace.

Improvement Tips

  • Solo Queue for Fast Improvement: Playing solo Q is one of the quickest ways to improve.
  • Respect Opponents: Understand and respect your opponents' intentions.
  • Stay Active: If you’re not on the ball, make sure you’re doing something productive.
  • Boost Management: Consider whether you have time to get boost, not just on defense.
  • Effective Rotation: Rotate back ball side but stay out of the way to remain close to the play.
  • Counter Attacking: Look for and maximize 2v1 opportunities to win games.
  • Boost Usage: Use boost primarily on offense; you can defend and recover with minimal boost.
  • Efficient Recovery: Save boost while maintaining speed during recoveries.
  • Momentum Management: Value and maintain momentum; it helps with speed and boost conservation.
  • Safe Second Man: Be reliable as a second man and position yourself to be back quickly after making a save.
  • Intention: Clear intention is crucial for success.

More Detailed Tips

  • If you want to break the fundamentals, you must know them first.

If there’s nothing you can do, move to where you think the next play will be or do something productive, like collecting boost pads or positioning yourself better.

Have Intention:

  • For example, say: “I’m going to go wall -> ceiling -> get reset -> get 1 touch on ball -> shoot.”
  • Stick to that plan—don’t switch up mid-shot.

Break mechanical skills down into small steps or drills to practice.

Don’t Reset Your Shot if You Mess Up—Try to Follow It Up:

  • Think about why you messed up and try to correct it on the next attempt.
  • Adapt to your mistakes during training and freeplay.
