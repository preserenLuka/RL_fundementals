Defense

YouTube Video

The team with possession is attacking, if they are not in control of the ball it's not an attack but still defense.

1st man:

  • Make 2nd man’s job easier
  • Make it hard on enemies on ball
  • Don’t throw yourself at ball (don’t leave your 2nd man in 1v2)

2nd man:

  • Create time for your 1st man to become 2nd man
  • Should use things that bring you the advantage: Take time, Fake challenges, good low 50s…
  • Don’t go if you don’t need to
  • Cover teammates weak outcome

1 should often be backboard (1st man or 2nd man?)

Counter Attacking

YouTube Video

Works because people overextend on offence because they don’t want to play defense.

You want to attack as quickly as possible, push the pace of the ball and make sure the last touch is perfect (take quick 1st touch).

You can shoot if they are too close a lot.

Clear it so it goes in the air.

When countering you can demo last if it's 1v2 or pass teammate if available.

When doing it go toward the net and not walls or corners.

Attack

YouTube Video

Don’t shoot at goalies.

If you pass don’t chase your own pass, but power shooting is important.

1st man:

  • Beat their 1st man
  • Make it awkward for their 2nd man

2nd man:

  • If 1st has 2 players to beat cover his weak (50 / outcome)
  • If 1st has 1 player to beat cover his strong
  • If your 1st is attacking you must be there with him even on low boost (collect some pads, 24 is all you need) (doesn’t mean overcommit)

Rotations and Recoveries

YouTube Video

Part of recovering and rotating is selecting pads:

That’s what makes you a fast player

Recoveries:

  • Recoveries are really important to get back as fast as possible but you should use as little/0 boost recovering.
  • You should try to save as much boost recovering and defending as possible
  • If you're close to supersonic speed feel free to use it.
  • With saving the boost recovering you have boost for offense

Rotations:

1st man:

  • If opponents are dribbling /on the ground, go harass them to make it easy for the 2nd man.
  • If they are in the air, it depends.
  • If you are fast enough rotate behind your 2nd man so that he can challenge from better position, and you can cover behind him.
  • If you won’t be fast enough, challenge in the air so you make it easy for your 2nd man.

2nd man:

  • If 1st has 2 players to beat cover his weak (50 / outcome)
  • If 1st has 1 player to beat cover his strong
  • If your 1st is attacking you must be there with him even on low boost (collect some pads, 24 is all you need) (doesn’t mean overcommit)

Speed and Decision Making

YouTube Video

Speed:

  • Try to always keep your momentum going / don’t break check
  • If you miss a boost, go past it most of the time (depending how much momentum you have)
  • How to improve speed:
    • Make decisions faster.
    • Doing simple things fast.

Decision Making:

  • Keep it simple / don’t overthink
  • Rotate back fast and safe, if you miss a boost go past it (don’t rotate wide)
  • Challenge the ball fast (speed and decision making)

Efficient Challenges

YouTube Video

It’s about closing the gap quickly and looking for signs of weaknesses.

Don’t get close unless you see something in your favor.

Don’t throw yourself at ball (don’t leave your 2nd man in 1v2)

Observe the opponent on the ball, what foot is he taking it on.

  • Is it going toward his goal (can you beat him)?
  • Has he outplayed himself (opponent made a mistake)?
  • Stay goal side
  • Make it look like you’re going to challenge
  • Force it to a place where you have advantage

Carry in Solo Q

YouTube Video

Why you should pass:

  • If you constantly go for solo plays, you’re easy to read and you make your own game hard.
  • Your opponents don’t know what to do if you pass, you’re playing chess and they play checkers.
  • Once you pass, the solo plays will become easier.

Why should you shoot a lot?

  • If you have the habit of not shooting enough, you’re making the job easier for the opponents

Why should you go for bumps and demos?

  • If you always go for the ball, opponents just wait for you, and this is predictable.
  • If you go for bumps / demos, it makes you unpredictable and gives you more space.

Positive Mental

YouTube Video

Try not to focus on bad teammates. Once you accept you can’t win all the games and are focused on improvement, the wins will come.

  • Stop having expectations.
  • Lower your expectations and don’t assume anything.
  • Focus on things you can change and forget about the things you can’t.

More Detailed Tips

YouTube Video

  • Shooting: most people shoot at the goalies and not away from the goalies.
  • Speed is most important: making the right decisions faster than the opponents will win you games.
  • Stop thinking you can control all the game and focus on yourself.
  • Instead of playing from boost to boost play from ball to ball.
  • In 2s you should look at the game like who is in front of you and who is behind you.
  • You should know what you can or cannot do when you are in front of your teammate and the same when you are behind.
